📖 Debrief — Path A
❌Wrong choice — here's why
The core lesson

In SAP, the material number is the specification. MAT-4471 and MAT-4472 look nearly identical in a search result — but they represent completely different technical grades with different performance thresholds. When materials share a name, the critical difference lives in the detail fields most people scroll past.

What Alex should remember: Speed under pressure is not worth sacrificing accuracy. A 5-minute call to the right person would have prevented a 3-day delay, emergency costs, and a damaged client relationship.

📖 Debrief — Path B
⚠️Good instinct — wrong person
The core lesson

Asking for help was exactly the right instinct. But the most available expert isn't always your manager. Diane has authority over the decision — she doesn't necessarily have the hands-on product knowledge to answer it quickly. When a question is technical and time-sensitive, go to the person who works with the materials directly.

What Alex should remember: Escalating upward isn't always the fastest path to the right answer. The warehouse team handles these materials every day. They're your most reliable source — and usually just one phone call away.

📖 Debrief — Path C
✅Right choice — here's why it worked
The core lesson

Maria answered in under 5 minutes and confirmed the correct material on the first call — without having to look it up. She already knew, because she works with these materials every day. When the question is about what's physically going to a site, the warehouse team is your fastest and most reliable source of truth.

What made this work: Alex recognized that he didn't know — and found someone who did. That's not a weakness. In SAP, submitting the wrong data is far more costly than taking 5 minutes to confirm the right one.

🎓 Key Takeaways

What Alex — and you — should remember

🔍

The number IS the spec

In SAP, similar-looking material numbers can represent completely different products. Always verify before you submit.

📞

Ask the right person

Your manager has authority — but the warehouse has hands-on knowledge. Know who to call for what.

⏱

5 minutes vs. 3 days

One quick confirmation call prevents emergency orders, expedite fees, and broken client commitments.

The big idea

In SAP, what you select is what gets ordered. Two materials with nearly identical descriptions can have completely different outcomes on site. When in doubt — before you hit Submit — take 5 minutes to confirm with the people who work with those materials every day.
